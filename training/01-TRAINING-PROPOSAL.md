## 1. Background

Following our visit to Unnathi CNC Technologies on 7th February 2026, we observed that your organization has made commendable progress towards ZED certification, particularly in establishing energy management documentation — Energy Policy, Energy Management Plan, consumption tracking records, and internal audit checklists.

However, we also identified that energy data collection and monitoring is currently being done **manually** without automated measurement devices. For ZED certification, it is critical that all personnel understand not just the documentation requirements, but also the **practical aspects** of energy management — how to systematically record, analyze, and act on energy data.

We propose a **one-day training workshop** focused on practical energy management skills aligned to ZED Parameter 12 requirements.

## 3. Training Objectives

By the end of this training, participants will be able to:

1. Understand the 5 ZED Energy Management requirements (12.1 to 12.5) and what the assessor expects
2. Identify all energy sources in the facility and understand consumption patterns
3. Record daily/monthly energy consumption data systematically
4. Set meaningful energy targets and track deviations
5. Conduct a basic internal energy audit
6. Raise and close energy-related CAPAs using the RCA methodology
7. Prepare for the ZED assessor's energy management evaluation

## 4. Training Agenda

### Session 1: ZED Energy Requirements — What the Assessor Looks For (45 min)
- Overview of ZED certification levels (Bronze / Silver / Gold)
- Deep dive into Parameter 12: Energy Management (12.1 to 12.5)
- What evidence the assessor expects for each sub-parameter
- Common gaps that lead to non-conformities
- How energy management connects to other ZED parameters (Environment, Measurement & Analysis, Technology)

### Session 2: Understanding Your Energy Profile (45 min)
- Energy sources at Unnathi CNC: Grid electricity, Solar PV, Compressed Air, Hydraulics
- Walking through your actual data:
  - Solar production: ~8,772 kWh/month avg (Jul-24 to Jun-25)
  - Grid consumption: ~17,084 kWh/month avg (Jul-24 to Jun-25)
  - Solar contributes ~34% of total energy
- Seasonal patterns and what they mean
- Understanding the relationship between production load and energy consumption
- How to calculate Specific Energy Consumption (SEC) — kWh per part/unit

### --- Tea Break (15 min) ---

### Session 3: Systematic Energy Recording & Target Setting (45 min)
- How to record daily energy consumption (meter reading methodology)
- Setting up an energy register — what to record, how often, who is responsible
- Setting SMART energy targets:
  - Baseline calculation from historical data
  - Quarterly reduction targets (3-5%)
  - Production-normalized targets (kWh per unit produced)
- Tracking actuals vs targets — deviation identification
- **Hands-on exercise:** Participants calculate targets from their own historical data

### Session 4: Internal Energy Audit — Practical Guide (45 min)
- What is an internal energy audit and why it matters for ZED
- Audit checklist walkthrough (using your existing checklist as reference)
- How to conduct a floor-level energy walkthrough
- Identifying energy wastage — common areas in CNC manufacturing:
  - Idle machine running time
  - Compressed air leaks
  - Lighting in unoccupied areas
  - HVAC inefficiencies
- Documenting audit findings properly
- **Hands-on exercise:** Mini-audit walkthrough of one section of the factory floor

### --- Lunch Break (45 min) ---

### Session 5: Root Cause Analysis & CAPA for Energy Deviations (45 min)
- Why RCA/CAPA is required for ZED energy management (Parameter 12.5)
- The 5-Why method — simple and effective
- Fishbone diagram for energy deviations
- **Worked example using your data:**
  - Deviation: Solar production dropped in Aug-24, Dec-24, Jan-25
  - Root Cause: Weather conditions (rain, cyclone)
  - Analysis: Is corrective action needed? When yes, when no?
  - Documenting the analysis properly
- **Worked example 2:**
  - Deviation: Grid consumption spike in Jan-25 (20,645 kWh vs ~17,000 avg)
  - Root Cause Analysis exercise (participants work in groups)
  - Corrective action planning
- How the same RCA/CAPA process applies across other ZED parameters

### Session 6: Energy Management — Digital Tools & Best Practices (30 min)
- Limitations of manual tracking — data gaps, inconsistency, audit trail
- Overview of digital energy management approaches
- Brief demonstration: How a digital system captures what the assessor needs
- Best practices from other manufacturing SMEs on ZED energy compliance
- Roadmap: Manual → Digital → IoT-enabled energy management

### Session 7: Assessment Readiness — Putting It All Together (30 min)
- Mock assessor questions and how to respond
- Document checklist — what should be ready before the assessment
- Roles during the ZED assessment (who presents what)
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Open Q&A

### Session 8: Feedback & Next Steps (15 min)
- Training feedback form
- Action items and follow-up plan
- Scheduling next engagement
