# SECTION 1: ZED & Energy Management — The Big Picture

---

## What is ZED Certification?

**Zero Defect Zero Effect (ZED)** — A Government of India initiative for manufacturing MSMEs

- **Zero Defect** — Products meet quality standards consistently
- **Zero Effect** — Manufacturing has minimal environmental impact

### Certification Levels:
| Level | Parameters | Assessment |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **Bronze** | Basic compliance across 20 parameters | Self-assessment + desk review |
| **Silver** | Intermediate compliance with evidence | On-site assessment |
| **Gold** | Advanced compliance with demonstrated results | Rigorous on-site assessment |

> **Key Point:** Energy Management is **Parameter 12** — one of 20 ZED parameters, and it directly impacts your certification level.

---

## Parameter 12: Energy Management — The 5 Requirements

| # | Requirement | What the Assessor Checks |
|---|------------|-------------------------|
| **12.1** | All energy sources identified & targets set | Energy source register, target documents, policy |
| **12.2** | Consumption measured & recorded; corrective actions on deviations | Consumption records, deviation reports, corrective actions taken |
| **12.3** | Employees trained in energy management | Training records, attendance sheets, effectiveness assessment |
| **12.4** | Periodic energy audits in place | Internal audit reports, findings, follow-up actions |
| **12.5** | RCA & CAPA processes in place | Root cause analysis records, CAPA closure evidence |

> **The assessor doesn't just check if documents exist — they check if the system is LIVING and ACTIVE.**

---

## How Energy Management Connects to Other ZED Parameters

Energy management doesn't exist in isolation. The skills you learn today apply across ZED:

| Skill | Energy (12) | Also Used In |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Target setting & tracking | Energy targets | Quality (5), Measurement (14) |
| Internal audits | Energy audits | Environment (13), Safety (3), Quality (5) |
| RCA / CAPA | Energy deviations | ALL parameters (5, 8, 9, 10, 12, 13, 14) |
| Training & awareness | Energy training | Safety (3), Quality (5), Environment (13) |
| Management review | Energy review | All — through MRM |

> **Learn it once for energy, apply it everywhere for ZED.**

## Setting Up an Energy Register

### What to Record Daily:

| Time | Reading Source | Data Point | Who Records |
|------|--------------|------------|-------------|
| Shift start | Main meter | Opening reading (kWh) | Designated operator |
| Shift end | Main meter | Closing reading (kWh) | Designated operator |

### Frequency of Recording:

| Data | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Meter readings | Daily | Trend monitoring, deviation detection |
| Solar generation | Daily | Renewable performance tracking |
| EB bill data | Monthly | Cost analysis, target tracking |
| Section-wise consumption | Weekly/Monthly | Identifying high-consumption areas |
| Specific Energy Consumption | Monthly | Normalized performance metric |

## Setting SMART Energy Targets

### Step 1: Calculate Your Baseline
- Use last 12 months average as baseline
- Your grid baseline: **17,084 kWh/month**
- Your solar baseline: **8,772 kWh/month**

### Step 2: Set Quarterly Reduction Targets

| Quarter | Grid Target (kWh) | Reduction | Solar Target (kWh) | Increase |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|--------------------|-----------|
| Q1 (Baseline) | 17,084 | — | 8,772 | — |
| Q2 | 16,570 | -3% | 9,210 | +5% |
| Q3 | 16,073 | -3% | 9,671 | +5% |
| Q4 | 15,591 | -3% | 10,155 | +5% |

### Step 3: Normalize Targets (Advanced)
- **Specific Energy Consumption (SEC)** = Total kWh / Units Produced
- Example: If you produce 5,000 parts/month using 17,084 kWh → SEC = 3.42 kWh/part
- Target: Reduce SEC by 5% → 3.25 kWh/part
- This is a fairer measure because it accounts for production volume changes

---

## Tracking Deviations

### When is it a deviation?
- Consumption exceeds target by **more than 10%** in any month
- Consumption exceeds target for **2 consecutive months**
- Any sudden spike (>20% from previous month)

### What to do when deviation is found:
1. **Record** the deviation (what, when, how much)
2. **Analyze** the root cause (see Section 5: RCA/CAPA)
3. **Act** — implement corrective action if needed
4. **Document** everything — this is your ZED evidence

# SECTION 4: Internal Energy Audit — Practical Guide

---

## What is an Internal Energy Audit?

A systematic check of:
- Are we consuming energy efficiently?
- Are our records accurate and up to date?
- Are there energy wastage points?
- Are we meeting our targets?
- What can be improved?

### For ZED: You must conduct energy audits **periodically** (at least quarterly recommended)

---

## Energy Audit Checklist — Section by Section

### A. Documentation Check
- [ ] Energy policy displayed and current?
- [ ] Energy source register complete and updated?
- [ ] Consumption records maintained (daily/monthly)?
- [ ] Targets set and tracking in place?
- [ ] Previous audit findings closed?

### B. Metering & Monitoring
- [ ] All meters functional and calibrated?
- [ ] Readings being taken as per schedule?
- [ ] Solar inverter readings recorded?
- [ ] EB bill data reconciled with meter readings?

### C. CNC Machines (Major Consumers)
- [ ] Machines switched off when idle for >15 minutes?
- [ ] Standby power consumption minimized?
- [ ] Machining parameters (speed, feed) optimized for energy?
- [ ] Coolant pumps running only when needed?
- [ ] No unnecessary machines running during low-production periods?

### D. Compressed Air System
- [ ] Air leaks checked and fixed? (Compressed air leaks can waste 20-30% of energy)
- [ ] Compressor running at optimal pressure?
- [ ] Air receiver tank pressure adequate?
- [ ] Blow guns used efficiently (not for cleaning where not needed)?

### E. Lighting
- [ ] All LED? (You have this — Yes)
- [ ] Lights off in unoccupied areas?
- [ ] Motion sensors installed? (You don't have this yet — OI)
- [ ] Natural daylight utilized where possible? (You have this — Yes)

### F. HVAC
- [ ] Temperature set at energy-efficient levels? (Your OI area)
- [ ] Filters cleaned regularly?
- [ ] Doors/windows closed when AC is running?
- [ ] VFDs functioning properly? (You have this — Yes)

### G. General
- [ ] Computers/monitors turned off after work hours?
- [ ] Energy awareness signage displayed? (You have this — Yes)
- [ ] Water pumps running only when needed?

---

## Conducting the Audit — Step by Step

1. **Plan** — Schedule date, assign auditor (should be different from the area being audited)
2. **Prepare** — Print checklist, review previous findings, carry measuring tools if needed
3. **Walk** — Go section by section through the factory
4. **Record** — Note findings with specific details (where, what, how much waste estimated)
5. **Report** — Write audit report with findings, categorized as:
   - **Conformity** — Meeting requirements
   - **Observation** — Minor issue, no immediate risk
   - **Non-Conformity (NC)** — Significant gap requiring CAPA
   - **Opportunity for Improvement (OI)** — Good practice not yet implemented
6. **Follow up** — Track closure of NCs and OIs

# SECTION 5: Root Cause Analysis & CAPA for Energy Deviations

---

## Why RCA/CAPA for Energy? (ZED 12.5)

The ZED assessor wants to see that when energy consumption deviates from targets, you:
1. **Investigate** why it happened (Root Cause Analysis)
2. **Fix** it (Corrective Action)
3. **Prevent** it from happening again (Preventive Action)
4. **Document** the entire process

> This is the **same process** you use for quality, maintenance, safety — "Same RCA procedure" as noted in your ZED documents.

---

## The 5-Why Method

**Simple, effective, and assessor-approved.**

### Example from Your Data:

**Problem:** Grid consumption in Jan-25 was 20,645 kWh — highest in 12 months (21% above average)

| # | Why? | Answer |
|---|------|--------|
| 1 | Why was grid consumption high in Jan-25? | Because solar generation was low (7,456 kWh — below 8,000 target) |
| 2 | Why was solar generation low? | Weather conditions — overcast skies, shorter days in winter |
| 3 | Why did low solar cause such a big grid spike? | Because production demand remained the same, so grid had to compensate |
| 4 | Why wasn't the production schedule adjusted? | Because there was no mechanism to plan production load based on solar availability |
| 5 | Why is there no such mechanism? | Energy consumption and production planning are not linked currently |

**Root Cause:** No integration between energy availability (solar forecast) and production scheduling

**Corrective Action:** For immediate relief — none needed (weather is external factor)

**Preventive Action:** Consider scheduling energy-intensive operations during high-solar hours (10 AM to 3 PM); review production planning to account for seasonal solar variation

---

### Example 2: When Corrective Action is NOT Required

**Problem:** Solar production below target in Aug-24 (7,498 kWh), Dec-24 (6,967 kWh), Jan-25 (7,456 kWh)

**Analysis:**
- All three months had weather events (monsoon rain, cyclone effect, winter overcast)
- These are **external, uncontrollable** factors
- Production from solar panels is inherently weather-dependent
- 9 out of 12 months exceeded the target

**Conclusion:** Deviation noted and documented. Root cause is external weather conditions. Corrective action is **not required**. Will continue monitoring.

> **This is exactly what your records already state** — but having it in a formal RCA/CAPA format makes it assessor-ready.

## CAPA Form Template

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **CAPA No.** | CAPA-EN-001 |
| **Date Raised** | DD/MM/YYYY |

| **Closed By** | Name, Date |
| **Reviewed By** | Management review (MRM reference) |

# SECTION 6: Digital Energy Management — The Path Forward

---

## Current State vs Desired State

| Aspect | Current (Manual) | Desired (Digital) |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Data recording | Paper registers, EB bills | Daily digital entry → automated |
| Target tracking | Annual review from paper records | Real-time dashboard with alerts |
| Deviation detection | End-of-month when bill arrives | Daily/weekly trend monitoring |
| Audit trails | Physical files, hard to search | Searchable, timestamped records |
| Reports for assessor | Manual compilation | One-click generated reports |
| RCA/CAPA | Paper forms | Tracked workflow with deadlines |
| Training records | Signed paper sheets | Digital records with certificates |

---

## The Journey: Manual → Digital → IoT

### Phase 1: Manual Entry Digital System (Immediate)
- Operators enter daily meter readings into a web-based system
- System calculates targets, flags deviations, generates reports
- All ZED evidence generated automatically
- Works on phone, tablet, or computer — no app installation

### Phase 2: Smart Metering (3-6 months)
- Install IoT energy meters at main load points
- Automatic data capture — no manual entry needed
- Real-time monitoring and alerts
- Machine-level energy tracking

### Phase 3: Intelligent Energy Management (6-12 months)
- AI-driven optimization suggestions
- Production scheduling based on energy availability
- Predictive maintenance based on energy consumption patterns
- Automated reporting to management
