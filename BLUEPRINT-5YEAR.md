## Year 1: First Revenue
### April 2026 – March 2027

**Theme:** Convert pilots to paying clients. Prove the consultant acquisition model. Build the sales playbook.

### Goals
- 10+ onboarded consultants (BEE-certified auditors, ZED consultants, electrical ESCOs)
- 50+ active client sites (IoT-enabled or paid bundle)
- ₹25–30L ARR
- First published case study (A Plus Fixtures — 3 CNC machines + solar, Bommanahalli)
- First ZED certification supported end-to-end on the platform

### Product Priorities
- **Consultant onboarding flow** — self-serve registration, guided client setup, demo data environment
- **WhatsApp alert integration** — automated notifications for deviations, CAPA due dates, compliance deadlines
- **Native mobile app (iOS + Android)** — begin development; PWA interim
- **BESCOM tariff calculator** — PF penalty pre-emption tool (highest ROI proof for consultants)
- **ZED certification workflow** — end-to-end checklist, evidence tracker, submission prep
- **Sensor onboarding UI** — guided setup for vibration, temperature, CO2 sensors per industry bundle

### Go-to-Market
- **Primary channel:** Direct outreach to BEE-certified energy auditors (list via BEE India)
- **Secondary channel:** ZED scheme consultants (MSME Ministry network)
- **Anchor partnership:** Lotus Controls — joint GTM with their Schneider hardware sales team
- **Events:** 2–3 industry events (CII Energy Conclave, BEE workshops, MSME expos)
- **First case study:** A Plus Fixtures savings proof — quantified ₹ outcome, published with consent

## Year 2: Proven Model
### April 2027 – March 2028

**Theme:** Demonstrate that the consultant acquisition channel scales. Build retention data. Prepare for institutional capital.

### Goals
- 50+ active consultants
- 300+ client sites (IoT-enabled)
- ₹1.5–2 Cr ARR
- Demonstrated consultant retention: 80%+ of Year 1 consultants still active
- 3+ published case studies across manufacturing, kitchen, healthcare verticals
- CCTS (Carbon Credit Trading Scheme) pilot with 2–3 clients

### Product Priorities
- **Native mobile app** — shipped iOS + Android; offline inspection support
- **ML anomaly detection engine** — moves from rule-based to pattern-based alerts
- **Carbon credit tracking** — ISO 50001 + CCTS overlap; baseline → measurement → credit estimation
- **CESC and MSEDCL tariff modules** — extend BESCOM logic to Kolkata and Maharashtra DISCOMs
- **Consultant referral tracking** — consultant-to-consultant referrals with commission tracking
- **API platform (v1)** — webhook and read API for enterprise integrations and third-party auditors
- **Two new industry intelligence bundles** — Textile Manufacturing, Food Processing
- **Bulk import** — CSV/Excel for historical energy data onboarding (reduces consultant setup friction)

### Go-to-Market
- **Consultant network effects** — referral programme live; each onboarded consultant incentivised to bring peers
- **DISCOM partnerships** — formal conversations with BESCOM, CESC, MSEDCL for co-marketing
- **OEM supply chain pressure** — target Tier 1 auto OEMs (Bosch, Toyota suppliers) to mandate VoltSpark for vendor ZED compliance
- **Enterprise pilot** — 1–2 multi-site manufacturing groups (5–20 factories under one organisation)

## Year 4: Horizontal Expansion
### April 2029 – March 2030

**Theme:** Extend the platform beyond manufacturing MSMEs into healthcare, hospitality, and commercial real estate — using the same IoT and compliance infrastructure.

### Goals
- 500+ active consultants
- 4,000+ client sites
- ₹25–30 Cr ARR (aligns with original SOM target)
- 3+ active international markets (Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, UAE pilot)
- Healthcare vertical revenue: 15–20% of ARR
- Platform marketplace: 100+ compliance service listings

### New Verticals (Years 4–5)

**Healthcare (NABH/NABH-accreditation driven)**
- OT/ICU environment monitoring (temperature, humidity, differential pressure)
- Critical load uptime + DG/UPS performance
- NABH-compatible compliance evidence generation
- Target: Private hospitals, diagnostic chains, pharma manufacturers

**Hospitality & Commercial Real Estate**
- HVAC efficiency, occupancy-based load management
- LEED/IGBC certification support
- Multi-property portfolio for hotel chains
- Target: Hotel groups, mall operators, office park developers

**Government & Public Sector**
- PSU factories with BEE Designated Consumer obligations
- Municipal corporation buildings with energy efficiency mandates
- Target: BEE DC scheme participants

### Product Priorities
- **Multi-region SaaS architecture** — data residency for international markets
- **SOC 2 Type II compliance** — required for enterprise healthcare and government clients
- **ERP integrations** — SAP, Tally, Oracle (energy cost data → financial reporting)
- **Regulatory submission automation** — BEE PAT cycle submissions, CCTS registry integration
- **Consultant marketplace** — facilities can discover and engage consultants through VoltSpark
- **Advanced carbon reporting** — Scope 1 + Scope 2 emissions, CDP-compatible reports

### International Strategy
- **Year 4 target markets:** Sri Lanka (similar electrical regulatory structure, active consultancy sector), Bangladesh (large garments manufacturing, BEE equivalent), UAE (DEWA tariff logic, ESMA compliance)
- Model: Local hardware partner + local consultant recruitment; VoltSpark platform as-is with DISCOM/tariff module localisation

## Year 5: Platform & Ecosystem
### April 2030 – March 2031

**Theme:** VoltSpark becomes an ecosystem — not just a SaaS product but the operating layer that connects industrial facilities, energy consultants, hardware vendors, DISCOMs, and government schemes in one platform.

### Goals
- 1,000+ active consultants
- 10,000+ client sites
- ₹60–80 Cr ARR
- Presence in 5+ countries
- Platform marketplace: 500+ consultant and service listings
- IPO or strategic acquisition readiness

### Platform Vision

**The VoltSpark Ecosystem by 2031:**

1. **Facility layer** — Every industrial facility, hospital, hotel, and office building has a live energy and compliance dashboard
2. **Consultant layer** — Every BEE auditor, ZED consultant, and ESCO manages their practice through VoltSpark
3. **Hardware layer** — Any meter or sensor brand connects via VoltSpark's open IoT API; certified hardware partners listed in marketplace
4. **Government layer** — BEE scheme applications, ZED submissions, CCTS credits — submitted through VoltSpark with one click
5. **Finance layer** — Energy efficiency loan eligibility, SIDBI/NABARD scheme matching, carbon credit monetisation — all driven by VoltSpark data

### Product Priorities
- **Open API platform** — third-party developers can build compliance modules, custom analytics, and integrations on VoltSpark
- **AI advisory engine** — platform proactively generates monthly improvement recommendations per client, benchmarked against industry peers
- **Automated audit preparation** — ISO 50001 surveillance audit package auto-generated from platform data
- **Carbon credit monetisation** — verified savings → CCTS registry submission → credit issuance tracking
- **WhatsApp Business integration** — fully embedded; consultants manage client alerts and approvals via WhatsApp
- **Embedded financing** — energy efficiency capex (solar, VFD, transformer) linked to SIDBI/NABARD loan schemes via platform

## Critical Success Factors

1. **Consultant adoption velocity in Year 1** — everything downstream depends on this. The first 10 consultants define the onboarding playbook.
2. **A Plus Fixtures case study** — first verifiable ₹ savings number, published publicly. This is the proof point that replaces 100 feature descriptions.
3. **ZED scheme tailwind** — government subsidies make certification affordable; VoltSpark must be the tool that makes it manageable.
4. **Lotus Controls partnership depth** — the consultant-recommends-hardware-deploys-VoltSpark flywheel must be proven in Year 1.
5. **Retention over acquisition** — a consultant who churns takes 5–20 client sites with them. NRR > 120% is the north star metric.

---

## What This Blueprint Does NOT Assume

- Guaranteed government contracts or mandates
- Viral adoption without a structured sales motion
- A competitor-free landscape (Tor.ai, Schneider/Zenatix, and others are active)
- That pricing will remain unchanged — the IoT meter and bundle pricing should be reviewed at each funding stage based on CAC and LTV data
